13.3 Quotient rule

There is a similar rule for quotients.

Para probarlo, vamos a la definici√≥n de la derivada:

\(\frac{d}{dx} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} =\)

\(\lim_{h\to0} \frac{\frac{f(x+h)}{g(x+h)} - \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}}{h}\)

\(= \lim_{h\to0} \frac{f(x+h)g(x) - f(x)g(x+h)}{hg(x)g(x+h)}\)

\(= \lim_{h\to0} \frac{f(x+h)g(x) - f(x)g(x) + f(x)g(x) - f(x)g(x+h)}{hg(x)g(x+h)}\)

\(= \lim_{h\to0} \frac{g(x) \cdot \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} - f(x) \cdot \frac{g(x+h) - g(x)}{h}}{g(x)g(x+h)}\)

Derivatives of quotients (Quotient Rule)

\(\frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{f(x)}{g(x)} \right] = \frac{g(x)f'(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{g(x)^2}\)
